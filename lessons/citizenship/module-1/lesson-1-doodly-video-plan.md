# Lesson 1 Doodly Starter Video

Lesson: Module 1, Lesson 1 - Who Are We?  
Video length: 45 seconds max  
Purpose: Open the lesson and get students thinking about identity.

## Video Title

Who Are You?

## Doodly Visual Plan

Scene 1: 0-7 seconds  
Draw a simple group of young people. Add the title: "Who are you?"

Scene 2: 7-18 seconds  
Draw one person in the middle. Around them, add labels: son, daughter, friend, gamer, footballer, Londoner.

Scene 3: 18-33 seconds  
Draw a phone screen with different app icons: music, family, football, culture, friends. Add text: "Identity has many parts."

Scene 4: 33-45 seconds  
Draw a profile card with three empty boxes. Add text: "Choose 3 words about you."

## 45-Second Voiceover Script

"Welcome to your first Citizenship lesson.

Today we are starting with one simple question: who are you?

That question might sound easy, but most people are more than one thing.

You might be a son, a daughter, a brother, a sister, a gamer, a footballer, a helpful friend, or someone from a particular area.

Your identity means the different parts that make you, you.

Think of it like your phone screen. Your apps, music, wallpaper, and messages are personal to you. Identity works like that too. It is made from different parts.

In a moment, you will choose three words or roles that describe who you are today.

There are no wrong answers. Just think: who are you?"
