# Scene 20 – Your Rights If You're Arrested

If a situation becomes more serious and someone is arrested, they don't lose all of their rights. The law continues to protect them throughout the legal process. For example, they have the right to speak to a solicitor before being questioned, and that legal advice is available free of charge. They can also ask for someone to be informed that they've been arrested. These rights exist to make sure investigations are carried out fairly and that everyone receives proper legal protection. Remember, being arrested does not mean someone is guilty. Only a court can decide that after hearing the evidence.
