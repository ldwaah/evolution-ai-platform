# Scene 11 – Protected Characteristics

The Equality Act protects certain parts of a person's identity, known as protected characteristics. These include age, disability, race, religion or belief, sex, and sexual orientation, along with several others. The law recognises that these characteristics should never be used as a reason to treat someone unfairly. Imagine being refused a job simply because of your race or being treated differently because of a disability. The Equality Act exists to prevent that from happening and to promote fairness across society.
