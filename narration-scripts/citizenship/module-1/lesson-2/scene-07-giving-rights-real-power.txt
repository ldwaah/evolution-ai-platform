# Scene 7 – Giving Rights Real Power

A right only matters if it can actually be enforced. That's why the Human Rights Act is so important. If a public body fails to respect your rights, there are legal ways to challenge that decision through the courts. The Act also influences how new laws are made, encouraging Parliament to consider whether proposed legislation respects people's human rights. In other words, it doesn't just describe rights. It helps make sure they have real meaning and real protection in everyday life.
