# Scene 21 – Legal Milestones

As you grow older, your rights and responsibilities change. At the age of 10, children in England reach the age of criminal responsibility, meaning they can be held legally responsible for criminal offences. At 16, you gain greater independence and additional legal rights, including opportunities to work and make more personal decisions. At 18, you become a legal adult. You can vote in General Elections, be called for jury service, and take on the full legal responsibilities of adulthood. Citizenship develops throughout your life as your rights and responsibilities continue to grow.
