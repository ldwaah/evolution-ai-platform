# Scene 24 – GCSE Exam Practice

Now it's time to think like a GCSE Citizenship student. Look carefully at the questions on your screen. Remember, examiners don't just reward students who remember facts—they reward students who explain, evaluate, and justify their ideas. For the 8-mark question, make sure you explain both sides of the argument before reaching a balanced conclusion. Support your points with examples from today's lesson. For the 12-mark question, you'll need to evaluate whether the Equality Act 2010 has successfully reduced discrimination. Think about the evidence that supports the law's success, but also consider the challenges that still exist today. The highest marks go to answers that compare different viewpoints before reaching a well-supported judgement. Whenever you're answering longer GCSE questions, remember this simple structure: Point. Explain. Evidence. Evaluate. Conclude. Follow that, and you'll be writing exactly what examiners are looking for.
