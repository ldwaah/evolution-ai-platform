# Scene 12 – Direct and Indirect Discrimination

Not all discrimination looks the same. Sometimes it's obvious, and sometimes it's much harder to spot. Direct discrimination happens when someone is treated unfairly because of a protected characteristic. Indirect discrimination is different. It happens when a rule applies to everyone but ends up putting a particular group at an unfair disadvantage. Understanding the difference is important because both forms of discrimination can have a real impact on people's lives, even when the unfairness isn't immediately obvious.
