# Scene 19 – Police Powers and Stop and Search

Let's bring the Rule of Law into a real-life situation. Imagine you're walking home from school when a police officer asks to stop and search you. Can they do that whenever they like? The answer is no. In the UK, police officers have legal powers, but those powers come with legal limits. In most situations, they must have reasonable grounds for suspicion before carrying out a search. They should explain who they are, why they're stopping you, and the legal reason for the search. The law is designed to protect both the public and the police by making sure everyone follows the same rules.
