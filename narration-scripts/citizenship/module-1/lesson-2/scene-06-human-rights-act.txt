# Scene 6 – The Human Rights Act 1998

So where do many of these rights come from? One of the most important laws is the Human Rights Act 1998. This law brought fundamental human rights directly into UK law, making them easier for people to rely on. It means that public organisations, including schools, hospitals, councils, and the police, must respect your rights whenever they make decisions that affect you. The Act helps make sure that people are treated fairly, lawfully, and with respect, no matter who they are.
